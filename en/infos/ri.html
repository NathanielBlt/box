Coming to Lille

Here’s all the info you need!

Residence permit

Before you arrive. Are you in France to study for longer than three months (and you are not from the European Union/Switzerland/Norway/Iceland)? You’ll need a permit: generally, this is the “long-stay visa for students”.
How to get it. For some countries, you have to follow a simplified procedure online (“Studying in France”). Otherwise, contact the French consulate in your own country.
Get your visa stamped when you arrive: you have three months to carry out this simple and quick procedure via the following website: administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr. This guarantees you authorised residency in France to complete your academic year. You will have to pay €60 and will need an email address, a postal address in France and a bank card.
After your first year in France, you will have to renew your visa to obtain a residency permit. You will have to provide quite a few documents … and have them translated into French (this is generally cheaper if you have it done at the registry office in your own country). Please note that you need to prove you have enough income (at least €615 a month).
Who should I contact? There is a department at the university that is used to all this, so you won’t need to do everything yourself. Feel free to get in touch!.

Help with the rent

You can have housing benefit to help pay the rent, called “personal accommodation benefit” (APL in French). But you need to apply as soon as possible because it takes a long time to process applications. You should contact a body called the CAF, and provide several documents (proof you are a student, proof of social security, birth certificate, etc.)

Opening a bank account

To receive housing benefit or get a mobile phone contract, you need a bank account: to open one, you have to provide proof that you are a student and proof of residence. Please note: ESN Lille has a partnership with the BNP Paribas bank, giving students a range of benefits.

Health costs

In France, the state takes care of a large share of your health costs: it’s called health care insurance. But you’ll need to sign up beforehand: everything is explained here (in French and English).
For all your administrative procedures (CAF, health insurance, etc.), be sure to register on our Livin France platform, which is free of charge and will ensure you adequately prepare for your arrival in Lille!

A useful association

Erasmus Student Network (ESN Lille) is an association that’s on hand to help you feel at home in France and organises evening events, visits to Lille, travel, etc.
