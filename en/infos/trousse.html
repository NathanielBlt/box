Got a problem or an issue?

Don’t keep it to yourself, there are people who can help!

The university is a big place and sometimes you may feel lonely. You need to be self-sufficient, make ends meet, and it’s not always easy. So if you have a worry, this is where you can go for help:

A medical emergency on the campus?

Don’t waste a second, dial 15. The emergency services are trained to react fast.
There is sometimes an emergency unit on the campuses: call them on 03 20 41 64 40 (Pont-de-Bois) and 03 20 43 44 44 (Cité scientifique). Ask at your academic secretariat or department/faculty (on other sites).

Feeling alone? Do you have health issues?

A medical, gynaecological, psychological, nutritional or social question or problem? Need a consultation, a medical certificate, a preventive check-up, a booster for a vaccination, etc.?
Go to the students’ health centre. There is a centre on five sites, with a single number: 03 62 26 93 00. You pay nothing upfront, you just need to show your up-to-date Carte Vitale health card (or certificate) and proof of health insurance, if you have one.
In conflict with your parents or issues with your partner? Contact the mediator from the Crous.
You can contact the university psychological assistance office (03 20 54 85 26), the psychiatric help centre on 03 20 78 22 22 (24 hours a day).
Feeling alone? Connect to Delcis platform : here you can discuss with students from Lille and help each other.
Please do the same if you see someone who looks like they need help or seems really down. A university is like a town; we need everyone to be a little bit attentive to the others.

Money worries?

Please don’t hesitate to meet one of the social workers. They are on duty in Lille, Villeneuve d’Ascq and Roubaix.
Discounted products are available in two solidarity grocery stores on Pont-de-Bois and Health Campus (open in October).
And for tips and practical information, feel free to visit the student-life office!

And don’t forget that your University is committed and civic-minded! It has put in place various initiatives such as bikes and computers that can be borrowed for free and even distributes free toiletry kits!

Harassment, discrimination, conflict?

The University of Lille is committed to combating all forms of violence and discrimination, and for equality between men and women.

If you have a problem or if you are upset by something, you can contact the mediation centre by e-mail, telephone or in person (by appointment). They can listen to you and guide you: the university has units to combat sexual harassment and bullying, and a mediator/contact for racism and antisemitism.

The University of Lille is committed to LGBTQI inclusion. You can ask here for a preferred firstname.

All these structures are committed to work in complete neutrality, impartiality and confidentiality.

Need some help?

Social workers, doctors, nurses, student-life staff, security guards: there are a lot of people on the campus to help you.
