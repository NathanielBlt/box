New services to help you!

You just need to go and see them…

Health issues, feeling down? The “school nurse” is now a health centre. You can go there without an appointment to see a nurse, get an appointment with a doctor or a gynaecologist (the pill, etc.). It’s free – you just need your Carte Vitale health card and your mutual health insurance card. There are several different centres and offices in Lille, Villeneuve d’Ascq and Roubaix.
Libraries. The “school library” has just got much bigger. There are several general libraries, and lots of other specialised ones. See more details on this page, and ask your questions on this chat.
Choosing the right studies. The “careers guidance office” at school is now the SUAIO. Do you have a career in mind or are you desperately looking for ideas? Want to change direction? Then go ahead and contact them!

What’s new

Registration and exams. For the administrative registration at the start of the academic year and for exams, there is an academic studies department. (LINK)
Erasmus and studying abroad. More information from the Direction of international mobilities.
Associations, accommodation, transport, etc. Contact the student-life and disability office. They will help you with practical problems (accommodation, transport, grant requests) and give information about setting up and running a student association.
Goodbye to P.E., hello to the sports department. At university, there’s a lot of choice – it’s free! The sports department has dozens of activities on offer.
Finding a job
After university, students find jobs in practically every field! Here, after a master’s degree, most students get a job within six months, and 80% within two years.
To meet up with professionals, do internships, etc. go and see the BAIP
To set up a firm, work in your association, create your own job – head for the hubhouse.

The student card: the vital sesame

A tip – always have your student card on you (= “CMS” card). It comes in handy for lots of things – as I.D., to borrow books from the library, to pay for meals, to keep track of your hours for sport and sometimes to access the work rooms, bike sheds, lockers, etc.

If we say one million, what comes to mind?

It’s the number of books/documents in the university libraries. In other words, lots of things you won’t find on the internet!
