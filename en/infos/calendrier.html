At university, there are two semesters

The academic year is divided into two terms or semesters. The first runs from September to December, and the second from January to June. (courses: about 12 to 15 weeks in each term.)
For bachelor’s degrees, the semesters are numbered. S1 is the 1st semester of the 1st year and S2 is the 2nd semester; S3 is the 1st semester of the 2nd year, and so on, until the last one, S6.
Sometimes people refer to odd-numbered semesters (S1, S3, S5 – the terms in the first half of the academic year) and even-numbered semesters (S2, S4, S6).
The exception is in the first core year of health studies (Paces), where there are three terms.

Course break ≠ holidays

Watch out for a misunderstanding: at some periods you don’t have lessons. However, these are not holidays, but “course breaks”. Roughly speaking, this means that you have times when you can relax, but also revise for exams, work on a project, catch up on missed lessons, etc.
A tip: don’t book your plane tickets – but wait for your faculty to give details about how exactly the course breaks are organised.
