What? Exams?

At university, you take exams - but not everyone!

Exams or continuous assessment?

How can you know if you can go on to the next year? It depends, either your marks from the whole year are taken into account (this is called “continuous assessment”) or you take exams, or there is a combination of both. To find out in your own case, ask your teachers.

Registration

Each year, you need to register several times:
at the university, at the start of the academic year (the “administrative” inscription (IA in French): for more information, contact your academic studies department)
for lessons, tutorial groups, etc. each term (“academic” registration or “IP” in French, in your faculty): it's important!
for exams: in some subjects, you need to register for exams each term

Exam periods

For the exams, there is generally one exam session each term, in January and May. And if you don’t get the average pass mark, you have a second chance at a “2nd exam session” (or “resit”), often in June for the two terms (in September for certain subjects).
The exam periods are given on the university timetable. The exact dates are announced 15 days beforehand in the campuses.
Please note, finding exam dates for 67,000 students is a real headache. So don’t be surprised if sometimes you have an exam on a Saturday or at less convenient times…
There’s no need to make elaborate calculations – working regularly and in all your subjects is the only way to pass your exams…
As for cheating – don’t take the risk, you’re not at school anymore. Cheating is an offence and leads to heavy penalties. This sometimes includes definitive expulsion from all the universities and schools…
