How to get here?

A few travel tips!

Lille has an international airport and several train stations with services to the rest of France and other countries (Belgium, Holland, Germany, the UK, etc.).
Lille also has excellent links to the rest of the Hauts-de-France region via the TER train network. Most of the time, you can take your bike on trains.
Each campus at the University of Lille is accessible by metro and bus. There is a map of metro and bus stops and nearby bike stations on the “Transport” page of the university website. On Cité Scientifique Campus, there is a Bike House (a community-based bicycle repair) (video, contact). If you don’t have your own bike, there is a bike-sharing network, V’lille.
There is a single travelcard for the TER train, metro, bus, tramway and V’lille: the Pass-Pass card.
So don’t hesitate to move around the different campuses for a meal, an exhibition, to borrow books, go to a lesson, walk in the parks, etc. – this is your university, so make the most of it!

Environmentally friendly transport

So you’ll see that we much prefer our students and staff to get around on foot, by bike and by public transport. After all, there are over 70,000 of us – if we all travel by car, imagine the impact on our lungs and on the health of all the local population (not to mention on global warming).
But if you really can’t do otherwise, several University of Lille sites have car parks that are accessible to students. Please note, they have a limited capacity.
