How to get here?

A few travel tips!

Lille has an international airport and several train stations with services to the rest of France and other countries (Belgium, The Netherlands, Germany, the UK, etc.).
Lille also has excellent links to the rest of the Hauts-de-France region via the TER train network. Sometimes, you can take your bike on trains.
Each campus at the University of Lille is accessible by metro and bus. Many bike stations are also available. There is a map of metro and bus stops and nearby bike stations on the “Transport” page of the university website. You will also find the journey times to reach the different university locations.
Need help repairing or maintaining your bike? Go to the Maison du vélo, near the Cité scientifique metro stop, for all the support you need! Alternatively, try the Jantes du Nord workshop in Lille.
Don’t have a bike? A network of public bikes (V’lille) is in place, and the University can also lend you one! (fill the form here).
There is a single travelcard for the TER train, metro, bus, tramway and V’lille: the Pass-Pass card.

Environmentally friendly transport

So you’ll see that we much prefer our students and staff to get around on foot, by bike and by public transport. After all, there are over 80,000 of us – if we all travel by car, imagine the impact on our lungs and on the health of all the local population. Not to mention on global warming: the transport sector contribute 60 % of University of Lille greenhouse gas emissions.
But if you really can’t do otherwise, use carsharing. Several University of Lille sites have car parks that are accessible to students. Please note, they have a limited capacity.
So don’t hesitate to move around the different campuses for a meal, an exhibition, to borrow books, go to a lesson, walk in the parks, etc. – this is your university, so make the most of it!

You will also find a wealth of information on this website that will encourage you to get out and explore the metropolitan area!

If we say 8, what comes to mind?

This is the number of towns where the University of Lille is located. Most of them are in the metropolitan area, but there are also others – Cambrai and Wimereux on the Opal Coast!
