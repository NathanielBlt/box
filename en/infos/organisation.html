How do the courses work?

Your goal: to develop a solid project. Your mission: to collect credits.

How to go on to the next year

Your year is divided into two terms or semesters.
To pass an academic year (for “licence générale”), you need to have an average mark of 10 or more in your "BCC"

BCC what? In order to build bridges between curricula, and to open university to people of every age, your subject is organized into blocks (bloc de connaissances et de compétences − BCC), each corresponding to a goal.

CU? UE? A course unit (CU) (UE in French) is a group of subjects (with lectures, tutorials, lab work), or a project, internship, dissertation, etc. You have about ten each term. One BCC = one or several UE.

There isn’t a system of compensation between semesters.

Build your career plan

In each term, there is a core course for all students, but also lessons to choose from, helping you to specialise through your choice of particular subjects.
Thinking about your career plan? You can precise it with option courses! See course unit Projet de l'étudiant (UE PE) here!
Often, what you do during your personal and professional project, such as your activities in associations, in the arts, sport, overseas and special CUs, can all count: get more information from your course advisors.

What is an academic unit?

An academic unit (composante in French) is the place where the various courses in your subject (bachelor’s, master’s degrees, etc.) are managed: the timetable, options, etc. This is where the administration in charge of your course is often located, the student affairs office.
The academic units, the education and research centres (UFR) and the faculties are the same thing. Sometimes, they have sub-divisions (departments). The name of your unit is given in the course file, in the “Contacts” tab.

What’s the deal with credits?

To complete a term, you need 30 credits – so, 60 for a year and 180 for the whole degree. Credits – also known as ECTS – are an equivalent grade you can use to study in other universities in Europe (Erasmus, etc.).
