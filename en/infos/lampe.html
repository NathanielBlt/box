School’s over!

At university, everything is bigger, but we’ll explain everything!

At the head of the university, there isn’t a headmaster or headmistress like at school, but a president. The current president is Jean-Christophe Camart.
Deputy headmasters or headmistresses are replaced by a number of vice-presidents or “VPs” in each field (research, training, culture, etc.). They are members of the teaching staff. They work to carry out reforms, set up new projects to improve the courses and the life of students and staff, etc. Like a mini-government!
But the VP you’ll be most interested in is the student VP, who, as the name implies, is a student!
And what about year heads? Well, there aren’t any either! You’re an adult, so you can discipline yourself. The university is also a way for you to become independent! On the other hand, there are safety officers to protect students and staff.

A young university

On 1 January 2018, the three universities in Lille – Lille 1, Lille 2, Lille 3 – merged. Today they form a single institution that includes 23 « composantes » (departments/faculties, school, institutes, etc.): the University of Lille and its 75,000 students!
