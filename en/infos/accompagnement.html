Need help for your studies?

For a top-up, help is at hand! So you might as well use it.

Let’s not kid ourselves – at university, some are more equal than others. They more or less know how it all works and set off with a bit of a head start.
So, to help compensate, the university and the public authorities have set up support measures adapted to your courses: mentoring, tutoring, refresher courses, individual support, work on spelling…
PRREL – who’s it for and why?
This was a scheme set up by the region and aimed first of all at grant holders, as well as students with a vocational or technical baccalaureate, and for students with lower pass-marks at the baccalaureate.
This measure is included in the “yes-if” option on Parcoursup. But it can also be suggested by your course leader at the start of the academic year to help you make progress.
It includes mentoring sessions, complementary elements designed by teachers, and a follow-up with an academic advisor for your project, for your options, for the direction you give to your education.
Made-to-measure orientation
Since the start of this year, made-to-measure courses that are better adapted to student diversity have been on offered from academic advisors – the “educational success contract”. It can extend bachelor’s degrees into a fourth year or may be a different way to teach certain lessons. But whatever happens, it’s the same degree and the same qualification at the end!
What is mentoring?
The idea is that students with a more advanced level (the tutors) work with you in small groups or individually, giving you tips about working methods, extra details about a particular point of a lesson and moral support to help you persevere…
Who can help you with your studies?
In most of the bachelor’s degree courses and technology degrees, you can regularly meet a teacher to see how things are going and get support, find ways to help you adapt and use the right methods…
You can also contact the health department, where there is stress-support on offer, with tips to take good care of yourself and stay in shape.
And don’t forget the student associations (students’ union, associations).
